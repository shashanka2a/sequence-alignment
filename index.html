What is Sequence Alignment?

Sequence alignment is the process of arranging DNA, RNA, or protein sequences to identify regions of similarity that may indicate functional, structural, or evolutionary relationships.

Learn More

Core Concepts

Global Alignment

Aligns sequences across their entire lengths, best for sequences of similar sizes.

Local Alignment

Focuses on aligning the most similar subregions, ideal for sequences of differing sizes.

Scoring Matrices

Used to calculate match, mismatch, and gap penalties in alignment algorithms.
